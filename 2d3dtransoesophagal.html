2-D & 3-D TRANS-OESOPHAGEAL ECHOCARDIOGRAM (TEE)

Seeing the Heart from Within

Trans-esophageal echocardiography (TEE) is a specialized ultrasound technique that provides detailed images of the heart similar to GI Endoscopy. A small, flexible probe with an ultrasound transducer is inserted through the mouth and into the esophagus, allowing a clearer view of the heart's structures (especially the posteriorly located ones) and blood flow.

TEE is particularly useful for:

1. Diagnosing Complex Heart Conditions: Such as heart valve diseases, congenital heart defects, etc.

2. Guiding Cardiac Procedures: Like in the deployment of devices in high-end ASD device closures, trans-cutaneous trans-catheter pulmonary/aortic valve replacement device implantation, etc.

3. Monitoring Heart Function During Surgery: Providing real-time information to surgeons.

TEE is a safe and well-tolerated procedure that can provide valuable information to help diagnose and treat heart conditions.
